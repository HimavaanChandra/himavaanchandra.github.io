Drones and RC Planes

In my spare time I pick parts, build, fly and race drones and RC planes. I have been doing this for many years as it is a big passion of mine. Building your own drone teaches you many skills including learning different electrical components and their compatibility with each other. It also allows you to pick parts for very specific purposes for example picking between efficiency or extreme performance. Building drones has greatly improved my soldering ability to make neater more solid joints. Finally, tuning a drone teaches you a lot about PID control loops and how changing each value changes the performance of the drone.

For the UTS Motorsports Autonomous team I have been programming a vehicle to race autonomously around a track. I first made a simple control program. I have done this by firstly, gathering information from a neural network about where cones are on a track. After this I pair cones on the left of the screen with cones on the right of the screen. From this I find the centre of each cone pair and then use a simple control program I wrote to make the car follow the centre path.
